  • Chapter 1

    • S.1 The Act is applicable to whole of India excluding State of Jammu and Kashmir (prior to abolishing Article 370 & RTI Act, 2009 is available in J&K till date).

    S.2 defines appropriate Govt.; competent authority; public authority; record etc.

  • Chapter 2

    • S.3 gives right to information;

    S.4 All public authorities disclose suo-moto information to minimize RTI Applications,

    S.5 provides appointing Public Information Officers (PIOs)

    S.6 provides filing of RTI Applications;
    > S. 6(3) transferring RTI Application to “another public authority…..as soon as practicable but in no case later than five days from the date of receipt of the application”

    S.7 disposal of RTI requests as expeditiously as possible and not beyond 30 days;

    S.8 PIOs should invoke the section if there is “unwarranted invasion of the privacy of the individual”;

    S.9 says that PIOs may reject requests, if RTI application infringes the copyright of an individual employee;

    S.10 empowers the PIOs to sever the confidential portion of information;

    S.11 is a procedure to be followed by PIOs related to 3rd party information who must be heard;

  • Chapter 3 (Central Information Commission)

    • S.12 relates to the Appointment of Central Chief Information Commissioner, Information Commissioners;

    S.13their terms of office and conditions of service;

    S.14: Removal of Chief Information Commissioner or Commissioner;

  • Chapter 4 (State Information Commission)

    • S.15 relates to the Appointment of State Chief Information Commissioner, Information Commissioners;

    S.16 their terms of office and conditions of service;

    S.17: Removal procedure of State Chief Information Commissioner or State Information Commissioner.

  • Chapter 5

    • S.18 (Complaint) If the RTI applicant has not received any response with in 30 days’ time, can lodge complaint to Central Information Commission or the State Information Commission;

    S.19 (1) Dissatisfied with the supplied information, RTI applicant may file 1st appeal to First Appellate Authority (FAA) within a period of 30 days from the date of receipt of the information;
    S.19 (2) Dissatisfied with the supplied information by the PIO; 3rd party may file 1st appeal to First Appellate Authority (FAA);
    S.19 (3) Dissatisfied with the supplied information; dissatisfied with the decision of FAA; RTI applicant, may file 2nd Appeal to the Central Information Commission/State Information, as the case may be; within a period of 90 days from the date of decision of FAA;

    S.20 Central Information Commission/State Information, as the case may be at the time of deciding any complaint or appeal is of the opinion that the PIO has not given information timely; has given knowingly incorrect and incomplete information, obstructed in any manner in furnishing the information, it shall impose a penalty of two hundred and fifty rupees each day till information is furnished, however the total amount of such penalty shall not exceed Rs. 25,000/-

  • Chapter 6

    • S.21 PIOs are given protection of his/her action if taken in good faith;

    S.22 RTI Act to have overriding effect over Official Secrets Act, 1923’;

    S.23 No Court can entertain any suit, no such order can be called in question otherwise than by way of an appeal under this Act;

    S.24 RTI Act doesn’t apply to certain organisations specified in 2nd schedule provided that the information pertaining to the allegations of corruption and human rights violations shall not be excluded;

    S.25 Central Information Commission or the State Information Commission, as the case may be as soon as practicable after the end of each year, prepare a report on the implementation of the provisions of this RTI Act (underlining is mine);

    S.26 The appropriate Government (Central or State) develop and organise educational programmes to advance the understanding of the public;

    S.27 The Appropriate Government (Central or State), by notification in the Official Gazette, make rules to carry out the provisions of the Act;

    S.28 empowers the Competent authority to make rules by notification in the Official Gazette, to carry out the provisions of the Act;

    S.29 Every Rule made by the Central government shall be laid, as soon as may be after it is made before each house of the Parliament, while it is in session or in two or more successive sessions;

    S.30 If any difficulty arises in giving effect to the provisions of this Act, the Central Govt. may make such provision but not inconsistent with the provisions of this Act and last,

    S.31 The Freedom of Information Act, 2002 was repealed.
